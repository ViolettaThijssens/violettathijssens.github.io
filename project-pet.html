Pet Food Shop

Quick Overview

Brief

The responsive website for a fictional local business lets customers browse pet food and place orders quickly and easily.

Process

My Role

Doing the project from start to finish, with the input of users and collaborators during usability testing and research.

Duration

As the project is ongoing, this is not yet known.

Success Metrics

I set these goals: sales/customer sufficient to run break-even in first year of operations and customer conversion rate of 3%,

Outputs

Prototype of responsive webshop for browsing and ordering pet food.

The Project Stages (Work in Progress)

Strategy

with Word, Photoshop and Manga Studio

Finding a unique selling proposition in the pet food market was a challenge. I decided to start small, so I opted to focus on one type of pet: dogs. However, the market was still big enough to harbour several types of businesses. This is illustrated below:

The results:

  • The entire local area had no dog bakery yet and it could have a good return on investment, so I went for setting up a dog bakery.
  • Customers can make one-time orders or set recurring deliveries

The positioning impacted the branding and set-up of the shop. For example, pictures of attractive food were more important than explanations of food properties.

Empathy Maps

with PowerPoint

I interviewed dog owners who did something special for the birthdays of their dogs in order to understand potential customers better. I distilled the interviews into empathy maps to organize what was said in the interviews.

Personas

with Photoshop

Based upon the empathy maps and the interviews, I created personas to have a good idea throughout the project what customers of the dog bakery website needed (goals) and what they did not not want to come across on the website (pain points).

Customer Journey Map

with Word and Photoshop

I also created a customer journey map to have an overview of the steps that a customer has to take to make a purchase. Consequently, I could ensure that I added all the necessary steps into the website.
